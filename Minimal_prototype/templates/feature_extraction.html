Feature Extraction Workflow

How Behavioral Features Are Extracted for Depression Screening

Step 1: OpenFace 2.0 Feature Extraction

From clinical interview videos, OpenFace extracts these session-level aggregated features:

  • Head Pose: Roll, Pitch, Yaw angles → aggregated as (mean, std, max)
  • Eye Gaze: Normalized (x, y) coordinates → aggregated as (mean, std, max)
  • Facial Action Units (FAUs): Intensities of 17 key muscle movements (e.g., AU04=brow lowerer, AU12=lip corner puller)
  • Blink Dynamics: Frequency and duration per session

Step 2: Quaternion Encoding (Our Innovation)

  • Head pose (roll, pitch, yaw) → converted to 4D quaternion: q = [w, x, y, z]
  • Preserves 3D rotational structure (avoids gimbal lock at pitch = ±90°)
  • Enables QSSA to capture psychomotor retardation patterns characteristic of depression
  • Clinical relevance: Depression manifests as coordinated 3D postural patterns (slumped torso + downward head tilt), not isolated joint angles

Step 3: Privacy-Preserving Transmission

  • Only the numerical feature vector is transmitted to screening server
  • Full video/audio remains on local device — never stored or transmitted
  • Participant identifiers are anonymized session tokens (no PII)

Privacy & Ethics Compliance

  • DAIC-WOZ Protocol: All data is anonymized per USC IRB requirements
  • No PII Storage: Participant names, DOB, contact info never collected
  • Local Processing: Feature extraction occurs on user's device
  • Data Minimization: Only aggregated numerical features transmitted
